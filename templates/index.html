Reduce unequal distribution between teachers and students in schools

Our Objective

Our Main Objective

To enable unemployed teachers, under graduates (pursuing education) to select desired places to go and volunteer to teach from different primary schools in Tanzania. According to recent data, there have been an increase in the number of students due to the policy from the government “Free education”, this has led to unbalanced workforce to the employed teacher (as they are unable to handle many students). Due to this, the ideal solution is to simply employ more teacher which is more of a policy (government) issue, thus a more ICT (Information Communication Technology) is needed to reduce unbalanced pupil to teachers’ ratio.

Background Information

The problem status

Pupils should have enough resources and infrastructures to help them acquire quality education as is their basic right, however most of the primary schools in our country face the problem of shortage of teachers which make the pupils not to acquire the good education and make pupils incompetent when they finish their schools

From the data from 2016 that showed the number of teachers as well as students in both primary school showed that, there was a total of 9,317,410 pupils in all primary schools and there was a total of 197,545 teachers in primary schools.

Also apart from the PTR of 2016 (Pupil to Teacher Ratio) the standard PTR is 40:1, but in Tanzania the PTR is about 257:1, this is obtained by taking the average ratio of the highest, the lowest PTR in the country as well as the standard PTR.

For example, according PTR report obtained back in 2016 it showed that in Kigoma, sokoine primary school had a PTR of 720:1 while in Ruvuma, Lunyere-asili primary school had a PTR of 3:1. This clearly show the unbalanced (unequal distribution of resource i.e. teachers in the country) ratio between teachers and students

F.A.Q

  • Who is capable of applying?

    As long as you are a graduate with passion or profession in teaching, you are eligible to apply with luck we will allocate you.

  • What are the requirements

    Our platform will ask you for the resume or Curriculum Vitae (CV) to store your information which will help us in validation. For a graduate we expect you to have your own details (certificate) on the day of reporting to the allocated school.

  • How long will it take until confirmation?

    It depends on the number of applicants and our help desk assistants, always it takes up to 3 - 5 week days to be verified.

  • How will I be allocated?

    Our platform will detect your location and allocate the nearest location if possible. In any cancellation or unsatisfaction you will contact our administrators.

  • Does it cover the whole world?

    Right now only graduates from Tanzania are allowed to apply, and will be allocated within the same country schools.

  • How will I be notified?

    Our platform will update your status to Approved in our system and we will send you an email with all your application details.
